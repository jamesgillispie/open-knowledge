# Website Thoughts — January 2026

**Early thoughts on website approach**

Sara Sartorius December 2025

**Moving into a different position with the combination of Taylor Wessing and Winston & Strawn, we may be required to create a new website to support the new entity.**

**With the transition from Taylor Wessing to Winston Taylor, we have a few challenges which we must take into account to ensure that we gain success of prime position within the digital landscape.**

**Key challenges**

Based on the data for the Taylor Wessing website, creating a new website will remove all the domain authority we have gained over many years with the current Taylor Wessing website. The following highlights the potential challenge.

**Performance of the Taylor Wessing website and some considerations around this.**

**Losing the [www.taylorwessing.com](http://www.taylorwessing.com) and adopting [www.winstontaylor.com](http://www.winstontaylor.com)**

Domain change and authority, Taylor Wessing to Winston Taylor. The Winston Taylor URL has only been in the public domain since December 2025; therefore, we will be working on a low authority level in search engines such as Google and Bing. Below is taken from Hotjar and shows the top referring sources to the website. 61.9% for Google and 5.5% from Bing.

The above image highlights the risk that 66% of the traffic to the site is a new user; Google's reliance will need to be supported by a paid search strategy.

This is how we mitigate any risk and ensure a smooth transition to maintain and build the traffic we have become accustomed to:

**The platform – needs to be right for our ambition and strategy**

1) Ensure we have the best possible website platform with the ability to host content such as thought leadership data, including podcasts, insights, brochures, onsite forms, high-quality imagery and video.
2) It needs to be easy, fast and secure to reflect the nature of the business
3) We need to continue to support a large amount of campaign content (hosting, measuring and building), as well as individual insight/ thought leadership content. We need to have a system that supports content control and publishing on the website with a feed to supply multiple platforms (LinkedIn, CRM and other areas of the digital estate).

**There needs to be a robust digital strategy and content strategy**

1) Supporting the new website with the content strategy, with a controlled and managed deployment of content within a good structural base on a high-quality website platform. This will need to be managed with care, selecting quality content which currently exists on the current website and migrating this to a new platform. The content audit will highlight the content worth keeping ie metadata and inform on key areas we can then use to populate the new website platform.
2) Content strategy to be put into place, which manages the timely deployment of content to ensure the organic traffic growth is built over time in an appropriate timeframe (to avoid any penalties from Google or Bing).
3) Process and timeline of paid media to support the new content deployment onto the website. This will scaffold the content to support the build of the new URL domain authority on search engines.

**The Platform**

From a previous investigation with UNRVLD, the costs were scoped out for a lift and shift approach for the current website onto two platforms. Optimizely and Sitecore.

In this scenario, there will be no need to consider the content migration; however, we would need to identify the content which is to be kept in place.

With these figures we can ignore the MVP build costs, as we assume we will be moving to a new supplier.

*These costs are based on quotations from UNRVLD May 2025.*

**Pros and Cons**

Below, I have taken the opportunity to review the pros and cons of each site; however, I can undertake a more in-depth comparison.

**Sitecore**

| Pros | Cons |
| :---- | :---- |
| Same as we are currently using, therefore, there only be a small amount of training required. | Not user-friendly and complex, takes more time to administer than other CMS |
| Customisation for complex and bespoke digital ecosystems | We don't use it to its full potential, therefore might be paying for something we don't need |

**Optimizely**

| Pros | Cons |
| :---- | :---- |

**Why SaaS**

* **Scalability:** Easily add or remove users/features as your business grows or shrinks, without investing in new infrastructure.

* **Automatic updates:** The provider handles all software updates, patches, and maintenance, freeing up your IT team.

* **Quick deployment:** No complex installations; you can start using the software almost immediately.

* **Reliability & security:** Vendors invest heavily in robust security, backups, and high uptime, often exceeding what individual companies can manage.

* **Integration:** Many SaaS tools offer APIs to connect with other business applications, creating seamless workflows

**The approach and structure**

**There needs to be a robust structure for the website with a rapid growth strategy for the audience, which we will have lost due to the domain change.**

With a new domain as highlighted above, there will be a lot of work needed to gain more content in the digital world and allow for more integrations, which will lend to a greater share of voice by using the algorithms of LinkedIn and, for example, Substack.

Structure for optimal reach and adoption of external content platforms to host large volumes of content.

**By creating this structure, this address:**

* Increases the viability of the brand by using the algorithms on LinkedIn and Substack to surface content
* To be able to store content outside of the website, which will support the storage capacity of cost and could support the speed onsite
* Allow the website to host important pieces of content without losing the support of the rest of the business, with the content being hosted on Substack and LinkedIn accounts, which are managed by individuals contributors
* This will enable the content team to surface specific content that may be useful for the strategy from these sources on Substack and LinkedIn for website, social and CRM use. This will also provide backlinking opportunities, which is beneficial for the domain authority.

**What are LinkedIn newsletters, and why are they important?**

LinkedIn newsletters act as an important thought leadership piece, which is visible to all followers on your LinkedIn profile. These can be easily shared on the social media platform by the main Winston Taylor account once approved by the administration/ content lead on the account.

This then allows some poorer-performing content to be managed by the authors.

How does Substack work?

Each Substack can gather subscribers and publishing insights immediately; this content can be submitted to the digital team for inclusion into the website; therefore, we can reach a specific audience but also echo this content on the website. With this link from Substack, this will support the SEO effort on the website but also build the personality of the content/themes on the lawyers' platform.

**How can we make digital cohesion**

* RSS feeds on the website and through Vuture emails for preferences.
* Key integrations with the website, including social media posts

**Strategic overview** propose adopting Optimizely as our core optimisation platform to enhance website performance, increase organic traffic through improved SEO, and underpin targeted digital advertising campaigns.

**Paid media & website traffic initiatives**

* **Three-Month (initial) PPC campaign:** Launch a focused pay-per-click campaign targeting Taylor Wessing brand terms in both the UK and France. The objective is to redirect high-intent search users to the new Winston Taylor website, capitalising on existing brand equity while supporting awareness of our rebranded offering.
* **Keyword strategy:** Employ brand-specific keywords and related phrases to ensure visibility where audiences are actively searching for Taylor Wessing, thereby capturing relevant traffic efficiently.
* **LinkedIn retargeting:** Activate LinkedIn's retargeting capabilities to engage a global professional audience familiar with our brand. While recognising the associated cost implications of this channel, we will closely monitor ROI metrics to ensure effective spend.

**Supporting SEO & ongoing optimisation**

The integration of Optimizely enables ongoing A/B testing and experimentation—key for continuous improvement in user experience (UX) and conversion rate optimisation (CRO). These activities will complement paid initiatives by maximising onsite engagement from all acquired traffic.

Further information on how Optimizely supports web experimentation can be found at the [Optimizely official website](https://www.optimizely.com/).

**Team dynamics & skills development**. To foster greater cohesion between design and digital functions, whilst reducing dependency on external agencies, it might be a good idea to look at the structural elements of the team:

**Current structure:**

* **International Digital Manager**: Strategy lead; oversees content management; manages UNRVLD partnership.
* **Senior Digital Executive**: CRM email/content editor; Web/email content editor; support paid media.
* **Digital Executives x2**: Web/email content editors; support SEO/campaigns; some paid media exposure.

**Identified gaps:**

* Advanced paid media expertise
* UX designer for CRO activity
* In-house website developer
* Robust analytics/tracking skills (GTM proficiency)
* Enhanced digital design skills

**Proposed future team structure:**

* **Performance Digital Manager**: Accountable for ROI analysis, strategic direction (across content, paid media), and reporting on all digital activity.
* **Content Manager**: Drives content priorities across channels (including social/LinkedIn/Substack/email); ensures timely, accurate delivery; collaborates with UX/CRO functions.
* **UX Designer Executive**: Responsible for web design excellence, CRO testing regimes, and CRM enhancement.
* **Digital Content Executives (2–3)**: Core BAU site/social/email production with cross-training in SEO/PPC/newsletter delivery.

**Capability enhancement recommendations**: To address current skills gaps:

* Invest in upskilling team members—particularly around advanced paid media management and digital design—to build internal capability where possible.
* Where necessary, recruit specialists (notably in UX/digital design/development) to facilitate more sophisticated experimentation/testing. This dual approach will enable more comprehensive web optimisation workstreams while reducing long-term reliance on agency partners—improving performance outcomes alongside cost efficiency.
