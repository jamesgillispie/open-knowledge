# Deck Outline

Where we are
Brief recap of the 2-week discovery, audit, and questionnaire. What we looked at, who we talked to, what we learned about both firms' content and editorial needs.

The hard constraint
May 1 deadline tied to the merger announcement. No flexibility. This is the lens everything else gets evaluated through.

Why Twill
AREA 17 jointly maintains it, dozens of production sites shipped on it with comparable complexity. Open source (Apache 2.0 / MIT), so no procurement cycle, no license negotiation, no contract review. Dev environment goes up the day the stack is approved.

The stack
Lean by design. Upsun for infrastructure (agency partnership), Cloudflare for CDN and security. Both proven, security-compliant. One contract, passes through AREA 17.

One team, one relationship
The operating model both firms described wanting. AREA 17 accountable for the whole thing, no finger-pointing between vendors.

Live demo
Walk through work already done pulling in data from the existing sites. Show that this isn't theoretical, the migration path is real and already underway.

Timeline and next steps
What happens between now and May 1. Key milestones, what we need from their side to keep it moving.

Appendix A: Security and governance
SOC 2 compliance as a hard requirement, SSO integration with Azure AD/MFA, security questionnaire already initiated. Stack chosen with both IT teams' vetting process in mind.

Appendix B: AI and AEO readiness
Not a Phase 2 nice-to-have, a design constraint from Day 1. WS prioritizing AEO over traditional SEO, TW wants AI-enhanced search near-term. Content model structured to be machine-readable from launch.

Appendix C: No throwaway work
Direct framing from the Feb 16 session. Every decision in this stack carries forward, nothing here is scaffolding that gets torn down in a future phase.
