**Context**

* WO approached by Meridian before Christmas, looking to merge
* One is a US firm based out of Chicago, string litigation background, a lot of trial lawyers, grew out of Chicago into a big force in the States
* TW based out of the UK with a strong German office and all across Europe. UK part of that business, including offices in Dublin and Dubai, that firm has a focus on IP, tech and emerging sectors
* Both firms wanted a more transatlantic footprint, were losing clients looking to do mergers across seas, didn't think these firms had the capabilities because they were regionally focused
* Felt like a good fit because of IP side of things, entrepreneurial business, a good jigsaw of two firms wanting to do the same thing
* Wanted a brand for when they would announce the merger
* Very ambitious, direct, wanting to feel like a different law firms
* Loads of these mergers are happening, it's not brand new, the approach
* Need to do something exciting with the brand to be stand out

**Strategic foundations**

* Normally firms focus on hero practice areas
* More exciting to focus on Who are we for: The businesses, people and markets driving global capital and innovation
* Leading with the client type was grounding
* Hero practice areas = Major Litigation, Critical Transactions (buying and selling companies), Strategic IP (idea ownership), Private Wealth (company founders, investors)
* Refined building blocks = where we feel there is something special about WO
* Unreasonable hospitality / Above and beyond service, a la Eleven Madison Park
* UK firm is very sector-led, in the industry - very important to clients

**Big idea**

* **Shoulder-to-shoulder law**
* Redefining client relationship, not ivory tower experts, we're doing this together
* Relentless type of client service and lawyering
* Strategy has evolved since this version
* Personalities: **Straight-talking, In the action, Formidable**
* The one in question: Formidable
  * Can we write to it, design to it? Is it actionable enough?
* It can feel one-note, brash, in your face, lacking nuance
* Trying to make it more nuanced and usable

**Territories**

* Territory 2 (not chosen): It was warmer and more traditional, graphically leaning on high-end, premium-need. They were really anti Territory 2.
* Territory 1: They were really into the attitude of this, and chose it. Got attached to the version they pitched.
* Voice is a key element: The attitude and personalities will come through in the voice
* **Logo**: Already delivered logo for them to start using even though the system isn't finalized. (not ideal)
* Caution around using shorthand WT wordmark: When space is limited (mobile website, social avatar), when addressing audiences that know WT
* **Purple square**: has become a defining element of the brand, they like the definitiveness, like the end of a sentence, a statement. Five ways to use:
  * As a period
  * As a container: for imagery, for headlines

  * As a motif, how it's used in infographics
  * As an organizer, how to highlight elements in text
  * Typeface leans into square footprint as a design trait
  * Could be a useful tool in the website
