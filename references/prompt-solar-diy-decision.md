# Prompt: Help Me Decide on Solar Panels for My EcoFlow DPU Setup

I need help thinking through adding solar to my existing home battery system. I'm not looking for a sales pitch — I want honest tradeoffs and practical advice for my specific situation.

## My Current Setup

- **EcoFlow Delta Pro Ultra (DPU)** — whole-home battery backup
- **12kWh total battery capacity** (LFP)
- **EcoFlow Smart Panel 2 (SP2)** — wired into my home's electrical panel for automatic backup switching
- **No solar panels yet** — currently charging the DPU from the grid only
- The DPU accepts up to **5,600W of solar input**

## What I'm Trying to Decide

I want to add solar to charge the batteries, but I'm torn between two approaches:

### Option A: Portable / Collapsible Panels
Buy EcoFlow or third-party foldable/collapsible panels (like the EcoFlow 220W bifacial or ecoworthy 130W flexibles) that I can set up in the yard when I want solar charging and fold away when I don't.

### Option B: Roof-Mounted Rigid Panels (DIY Install)
Buy standard rigid solar panels and mounting brackets, and install them on my roof myself. No professional installer — I'd do the mounting, wiring, and connection to the DPU myself.

## What I Need From You

1. **Honest comparison of both options** for my specific setup — cost per watt, realistic daily output, how much of my 12kWh I could actually fill, and total system cost for each approach
2. **Practical considerations I might be missing** — permits, inspections, roof penetration risks, insurance implications, HOA issues, cable routing from roof to DPU, etc.
3. **What size system makes sense?** Given 12kWh of storage and 5,600W max solar input, what's the sweet spot in panel wattage for each option? I don't need to max out the input — what gets me the best ROI?
4. **The "do nothing" option** — is time-of-use arbitrage (charging batteries on cheap off-peak grid rates, discharging during peak) good enough that solar panels aren't worth the hassle? What would my utility rate structure need to look like for this to pencil out?
5. **A third option I might not be considering** — ground mount in the yard, awning/patio cover mount, or some hybrid approach where I start portable and upgrade later?
6. **Specific product recommendations** — what panels, what mounting hardware, what wiring/connectors, and rough total cost for a practical setup in each scenario

## Context That Might Matter

- I'm comfortable with basic electrical work and DIY projects
- I own my home
- I'm in the US
- My primary goal is reducing my electric bill and maximizing the value of the battery system I already own
- Secondary goal is energy independence / grid resilience
- I don't want to deal with net metering or selling back to the utility — this is purely for self-consumption and battery charging

Be direct. If one option is clearly better for my situation, say so and explain why. If the answer is "it depends," tell me what it depends on.
