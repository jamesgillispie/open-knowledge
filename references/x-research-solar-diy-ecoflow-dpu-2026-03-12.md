## Synthesis: What This Means for Your Decision

### The portable/collapsible argument
- **Pro:** Flexibility, no roof penetrations, take it with you if you move, deploy only when needed, no permits
- **Con:** Friction — you have to set them up every time. @OlaoluN1 bought a foldable panel and never uses it. Real-world output is lower (not optimal angle, not always deployed)
- **Best for:** If you rent, might move, or want solar for camping/RV/emergency use too

### The roof mount argument
- **Pro:** Set-and-forget, optimal angle, maximum output per watt of panel, long-term ROI
- **Con:** Roof penetrations, potentially needs permits, harder to move, more upfront work
- **Best for:** If you own the home, plan to stay, and want to maximize your 12kWh battery investment

### What X isn't telling you (but forums/YouTube will)
- The DPU accepts up to **5,600W of solar input** — that's a LOT of panel capacity. Portable panels won't come close to filling that. Even 4x 400W rigid panels (1,600W) would meaningfully charge your 12kWh in a few hours of good sun.
- The $/watt comparison matters: rigid panels are ~$0.50-0.80/W, EcoFlow portable panels are ~$1.00-1.50/W. You pay a significant premium for portability.
- Your SP2 is already wired for whole-home backup — roof panels feeding the DPU through SP2 is the natural completion of that system.
