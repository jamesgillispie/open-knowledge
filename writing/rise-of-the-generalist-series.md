# Rise of the Generalist — Series Plan

A series about staying human in the age of AI, the value of depth, and what happens when you carry mastery across boundaries.

This is the hub for the series. The three drafts are linked below.

---

## Published / Drafted

### 01 — Rise of the Generalist
- **Status:** Draft complete
- **Core idea:** Knowing what mastery feels like is the generalist's edge. Depth transfers. AI commoditizes the first 80% — what's left is taste, judgment, integration.
- **Draft:** [01 - Rise of the Generalist](./01-rise-of-the-generalist.md)

### 02 — The Terminal Has Gone Mainstream
- **Status:** Draft complete
- **Core idea:** The splitting of the atom rewrote perception; modernism/Cubism was the cultural response. AI does the same for computing — the terminal (direct instruction to machines) is now accessible to everyone. The generalist composes the fragments.
- **Draft:** [02 - The Terminal Has Gone Mainstream](./02-the-terminal-has-gone-mainstream.md)

### 03 — The Rubber Duck and the Night
- **Status:** Draft complete
- **Core idea:** AI is the new rubber duck — an externalization surface. Run thinking through multiple lenses, separate the concerns of generation and judgment, and let time (the collaborator you can't automate) do its work.
- **Draft:** [03 - The Rubber Duck and the Night](./03-the-rubber-duck-and-the-night.md)

## Future Post Ideas

- **The 20% That Remains** — What judgment, taste, and integration actually look like in practice. Concrete examples from design, engineering, and painting.
- **Foundation Before Surface** — The discipline of doing the hard things first. Stretchers, primed canvases, accessibility, design systems. Why what you build on determines everything.
- **The Music Conservatory** — Growing up at a music conservatory in Houston. How early exposure to disciplined practice shaped everything after.
- **Orchestration as a Skill** — The generalist as conductor. How composing across tools, teams, and disciplines is its own mastery.
- **Creation Is an Action** — Showing up before inspiration arrives. The practice of making things vs. waiting for permission.

---

## Series Voice & Constraints

- Essayistic, tight. No filler.
- First person. Draw from painting, design, engineering, music.
- ~1,200–1,500 words per post.
- Each post stands alone but threads back to the generalist thesis.
- Close each post with series tagline.
