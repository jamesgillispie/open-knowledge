## AREA 17 — Paris Leadership Summit

Leadership summit in Paris all week (Mike, Mark, Gemma, Bino, Kemp, Citrine, Marius, and others). Four threads surfaced in Friday's engineering board session.

**Pod model**: moving from discipline-based teams to practice areas (strategy + technology + engineering combined). A pod is a stable cross-functional team of ~10 that owns a project from BD through delivery, self-manages casting and handoffs, and can carry two projects concurrently given the natural availability gaps between project phases. Designed to address the recurring retrospective complaints: being added late, no context on estimates, split focus across two projects. Goal is to have a clearer picture in place by end of year.

**Skill matrix**: replacing the current 360/Lattice process with structured levels and clear expectations per role. Transparent progression criteria, better coaching tools for managers.

**Engineering board**: agents.md PR (large change adding Composer lint, format, PHPStan instructions — needs backend review from Sohere before merge); git workshop and vibe coding template (Luis drafting early August, September rollout, React/pnpm/ARE 17 standards); env file standardization (Luis seeking feedback on the ticket — read it, comment if it fits, flag anything missing); Twill Image class helpers (blade components unused, Luis to investigate merging into Twill core via Quentin and the Twill Slack channel); test suites (Tim and Clara working through Playwright and Pest with Pablo, Slack channel open to others).

**Responsible AI usage**: two explicit policy asks — don't use AI to draft Slack messages or emails where a human response is expected; don't send AI-generated output you haven't reviewed. More to come at the next Show and Tell. George also gave his SXSW behavioral branding talk Thursday. Core argument: brand is behavior, not expression; AI shifts brand competition from expression to evidence. Nike is named in 91% of athletic footwear answers but recommended only 28% of the time — a 63-point gap expression alone can't close. Raised the need for AREA 17 to develop a formal position on AI use, covering efficiency vs. knowledge advancement vs. client advisory roles.

## Analytics & AI Tools

**Nexus (Pinecone)** — June 23 demo with Jasmine (Head of Product) and Lucas (Account Manager). Nexus compiles raw data into structured "knowledge artifacts" via a curation runtime, then queries them via NoQL (a declarative language specifying output shape, grounding, budget, and scope). Claims ~94% higher accuracy vs. simple RAG on the same corpus, lower token use, lower latency — Pinecone cites the advantage as pre-built knowledge rather than reconstructing it from chunks at query time. Use cases discussed: an internal knowledge base (IP currently scattered across Slack, Figma, Google Drive, BD responses); James Street personalization (first-party data + site content → contextual prompts per user); and Pentagram archive search upgrade to cut token cost. James to write up 1–2 use cases with identified datasets; Jasmine will configure a dedicated test environment.

**Snowflake / Pliable** — June 25 call with Nicole (Snowflake) and Patrick (Pliable). Pliable is an LLM chatbot ("Ellie") governed by a semantic layer built entirely within Snowflake — no data leaves to external LLMs, 99.9% repeatable accuracy claimed, flags unanswerable questions rather than hallucinating. Snowflake now supports both OLTP and OLAP natively via its Crunchy Data acquisition, eliminating the brittle ETL pipelines previously required to bridge the two. Snowflake also handles vector retrieval natively, potentially replacing a separate Pinecone vector store. The call ended early due to a bot attack on a live client site; James committed to continuing the conversation with Nicole.

**Claude + Slack / BD automation** — June 25 session. Anthropic's Claude Tag Slack integration is being trialed with $2,500 in free credits; attractive because it stays inside the existing Slack/Claude ecosystem without adding a new tool to manage (Dobbin evaluated but ~$5k/month for the tier needed). BD automation concept brainstormed: HubSpot entry triggers Slack BD channel creation, Google Drive folder, Scoro placeholders, and an automated audit tool; Claude Tag handles routing (brand vs. technical) and invites the right people. A governance layer and check-ins with ops (Citrine, Mary) are needed before agents run autonomously.

**FTZ / GTM** — June 25 call. Microsoft Clarity was masking content by default (AI-based sensitivity detection), especially on healthcare-related projects; fix is to disable masking per project in settings. Projects to audit: Pentagram, National Academies, VMFA, and any others. FTZ should be standard practice on every new engagement, introduced at contract stage with a boilerplate GTM permission letter framing it as "to improve your X, we'd like permission to implement GTMs for tracking." Mike may have already started a draft in Confluence. FTZ and GTM integration for Nation is on hold until after the week of July 6 when things settle.
