# AREA 17 Transformation (Operating System / Pods)

A multi-quarter organizational shift at [AREA 17](/clients/area-17/overview.md) from grouping people by skill to grouping them by outcome — "We used to be grouped by skills. Now we're grouped by outcome." The transformation is [George](/people/george-a17.md)'s response to an AI-disrupted agency landscape, with the stated thesis that industrialization fractured the flow of intelligence (sensing → analysts, making → workers, learning → managers) and the goal of restoring that flow so people who sense can act, makers can redirect, and learners can influence.

## Status

In progress (Q2 2026). Org-structure implementation underway; pod pilot targeted for Q4 2026 or January 2027 at the latest.

## Roadmap

- **Q1** — Launched the operating system; introduced practice and service areas.
- **Q2 (current)** — Implementing org structure: team titles and a skills matrix launching soon; a leadership summit in Paris kicks off pod exploration.
- **Q3** — Define pod strategy, build pod team composition, identify role/skill gaps.
- **Q4 / Jan 2027** — Activate the pod pilot.

Under the new model, pods own conversion/outcomes via a two-phase handoff: strategy leads on vision and possibilities, then product directors take over for delivery and ROI. Early signals are positive — more autonomy to decline misaligned BD, structure engagements differently, and experiment on projects. The current challenge is not clarity on the transformation but getting people to ask for help to reach it.

## Margin discipline

A core operational thread. AREA 17's margins run 17–18% vs. an industry-standard 25–35% — a gap driven not by capacity but by **over-servicing**. The new "shaper" tool is reframed as a budgeting tool that sets a post-calibration **target margin** requiring an explicit conversation to change; over-servicing can be a deliberate account decision but never the default. See [Margin / Over-Servicing Discipline](/concepts/margin-over-servicing.md). Casting and planning move to Scoro; a no-timesheets initiative is underway.

## Key decision

**Make [FTZ analytics / GTM tracking](/projects/ftz-analytics.md) standard practice for every new engagement**, gated by a reusable boilerplate permission letter sent at the contract stage — framing tracking as a client benefit and securing buy-in early.
