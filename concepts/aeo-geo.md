# AEO/GEO & AI as a Site Visitor

A strategic framing that recurs across pitches and engagements: AI agents (ChatGPT, Claude, and their crawlers) are now a primary class of site visitor, not an afterthought. Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) reframe the question from "how do humans find this content" to "how does an AI find, parse, cite, and represent this content."

## The idea

If a meaningful share of audiences now encounter a brand's expertise through an AI intermediary rather than a Google results page, then content has to be structured to be machine-legible and AI-citable. This shows up concretely in the [Truth Initiative Pitch](/projects/truth-initiative-pitch.md), in [Winston Taylor](/clients/winston-taylor/overview.md) thinking, and in the [NASEM Site](/projects/nasem-site.md) (where an internal "Betty" AI concept came up as a way to make institutional knowledge addressable).

## Why it recurs

It is the strategic counterweight to [Bot Management / Edge & SEO Trade-offs](/concepts/bot-management-seo.md): the same engagements that want to *block* low-quality bots also want to be *discoverable* by the AI agents that matter. The two themes are two halves of one question — which automated visitors do you welcome, and how do you serve them.

## Key tensions

- **Citability vs. proprietary methodology.** Organizations want their content surfaced and cited by AI, but not their proprietary methodology exposed or commoditized. Structuring content for AEO/GEO means deciding what to make machine-legible and what to protect.
- **ChatGPT visibility vs. owned-site traffic.** Surfacing content inside ChatGPT may satisfy the user without ever driving them to the owned site — a direct tension between reach/authority and the owned-channel engagement metrics most clients still measure on.
- **AI as a first-class persona.** The reframe is to treat an AI agent as a named visitor persona with its own needs (structured data, clean semantics, clear citations), designed for alongside human personas.

## Through-line

The consistent position is that AI is now an essential site visitor, and that brands must deliberately design for AI discoverability and citation while making conscious choices about what proprietary value to protect.
