# Margin / Over-Servicing Discipline

The studio's margins run **17-18%**, well below the industry standard of **25-35%**. The recurring insight is that this gap is **not a capacity problem** — utilization is actually strong (78% average across both studios in H1). It is a **pricing-and-scoping discipline problem**: teams absorb cost by quietly over-servicing accounts rather than holding the line on scope, so margin leaks out at delivery even when the team is busy.

## The idea

Over-servicing is the default failure mode of a craft-led studio: a team that cares about the work keeps polishing past the point the budget pays for. Done unconsciously and repeatedly, it compresses margin across the whole portfolio. The fix is not "work less" — it is to make the target margin an **explicit, defended number**, and to make any decision to spend below it a **conscious one** rather than an accident of good intentions.

The mechanism is a budgeting / shaper tool (part of the AREA 17 Transformation) that, post-calibration, sets a **target margin that requires an explicit conversation to override**. The number becomes a tripwire: a team can choose to go below it, but only deliberately and with sign-off. Over-servicing is then reframed from a silent default into a **deliberate account decision** — sometimes the right call for a strategic relationship, but never the unexamined norm.

## The numbers

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Current studio margin | 17-18% |
| Industry standard | 25-35% |
| H1 utilization | 78% (strong) |
| Budgeting logic | Budget at 25-35% to land at 15-20% net after overages and discounts |

The budgeting logic is the operational heart of it: you **budget high (25-35%) precisely because real-world overages and discounts erode the number on the way down**. Pricing to the target you actually want to net produces a project that finishes underwater. Pricing to 25-35% gross is what lands a healthy 15-20% net.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The point is not to stop over-servicing entirely. It is to convert it from an invisible, portfolio-wide margin leak into a visible, per-account choice that someone has to argue for.

## Why it recurs

This sits underneath the broader AREA 17 Transformation. Strong utilization with weak margin is the diagnostic signature that the problem is structural, not effort-based — which is exactly why the answer is a tooling and governance change (the shaper's defended target margin) rather than a push for more billable hours. It connects to the studio's wider tension between craft and commercial discipline, and to how the new pod structure and estimation tooling are meant to make scope and margin legible by default.
