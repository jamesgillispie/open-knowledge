# Content Migration, Taxonomy & CMS Templating

The [Winston Taylor MVP](/projects/winston-taylor-mvp.md) (Project Meridian) crystallized a recurring class of work: migrating a large body of legacy content into a new CMS while rationalizing its structure rather than copying its mess. The migration covers roughly 1,053 items spanning insights, news, and events.

## What the work involves

- **Rationalizing content types.** Deciding which content types genuinely exist (insights vs. news vs. events) rather than carrying forward every legacy distinction.
- **Taxonomy design.** Establishing the cross-cutting taxonomy — sectors, practice areas, regions, topics — that lets content be filtered, related, and surfaced consistently.
- **Series vs. standalone.** Modeling content that belongs to a series distinctly from one-off standalone pieces.
- **Asset and imagery cleanup.** Confronting low-resolution and stock imagery inherited from the legacy estate.
- **Legacy-firm disclaimers.** Handling disclaimers and attributions tied to the predecessor firm's content.

## The aspirational end state

The target is an in-CMS "super template" — a flexible, component-driven page template inside the CMS expressive enough to replace content currently produced off-platform in [Foleon](/clients/foleon/overview.md). The goal is to bring richly designed long-form content back onto the owned platform instead of an external publishing tool.

## Why it recurs

Every platform replacement forces the same reckoning: legacy content was authored under old assumptions, and a clean new CMS is only as good as the taxonomy and templates the content lands in. Migration is where information architecture, design system, and editorial workflow all have to agree at once — which is why it is treated as a strategic exercise, not a copy-paste task.

## Through-line

Migration is an opportunity to fix structure, not just move bytes: rationalize the types, design the taxonomy deliberately, and build templates flexible enough to retire off-platform tools.
