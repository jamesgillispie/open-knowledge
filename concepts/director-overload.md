# Director Overload & IC vs. Strategist Tension

A recurring personal and operational theme: [James](/people/james-gillispie.md) — and alongside him [Nikki](/people/nikki.md) and [Ashley](/people/ashley.md) — is stretched across multiple live projects plus business development, repeatedly asked to operate at two altitudes at once.

## The tension

The core conflict is being expected to be both the "30,000-foot" BD and strategy voice *and* the "in the trenches" technical director on the same week, on multiple accounts simultaneously. The individual-contributor pull (writing code, hands-on TD, picking up dropped balls) competes directly with the strategist/leadership pull (BD, account strategy, team direction). Doing both well at the same time is structurally hard, and it surfaces as a capacity and focus problem across engagements like the [Jane Street Website Redesign](/projects/jane-street-redesign.md) and the [NASEM Site](/projects/nasem-site.md).

## Why it recurs

It is a symptom of a small senior bench carrying a wide portfolio. When the same few people are the strategic face *and* the delivery backstop, every new project deepens the overload rather than distributing it.

## Responses and advice

- **Delegation to [Patrick](/people/patrick-a17.md)** to offload technical-director and delivery load.
- **A new PM hire** to absorb project-management overhead that was landing on senior leads.
- **"Don't pick up every dropped ball"** — explicit advice to resist the IC reflex of catching everything, which keeps senior people in the weeds and prevents them from operating at the altitude the business actually needs from them.

## Through-line

The healthy resolution is structural, not heroic: build the bench and the process (delegation, PM, hiring) so that senior people can choose their altitude deliberately instead of being pulled to both extremes at once. It connects to [Margin / Over-Servicing Discipline](/concepts/margin-over-servicing.md) — over-servicing is often what overloads the directors in the first place.
