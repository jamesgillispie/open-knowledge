# Analytics as an Engagement Through-Line

A strategic shift: moving analytics from an informal, post-launch afterthought into a purpose-built through-line designed into every engagement from the start.

## The shift

Historically, analytics showed up late — a dashboard bolted on after launch. The new posture treats measurement as a first-class deliverable woven through the whole engagement, so that what gets built is instrumented intentionally and tied back to business outcomes. The [FTZ Analytics / GTM Program](/projects/ftz-analytics.md) is the clearest expression, with the [NASEM Site](/projects/nasem-site.md) another applied case.

## What it looks like in practice

- **FTZ click tracking with clean AREA 17 GTM event names** — a disciplined, consistently-named event taxonomy in Google Tag Manager rather than ad-hoc tags.
- **Custom DOM event matrices** — mapping interactive elements to tracked events systematically across a site.
- **BigQuery / Looker** — piping events into a warehouse and BI layer for real analysis rather than just a tag-manager dashboard.
- **Campaign-to-conversion attribution** — connecting marketing campaigns through to conversions so spend can be tied to outcomes.

## Why it recurs

Clients increasingly judge digital work by measurable outcomes, not just launch. Designing the measurement layer up front — clean event naming, a DOM event matrix, warehouse + BI, attribution — turns analytics from a reporting chore into a strategic asset and a reason to stay engaged.

## Through-line

Instrument deliberately from day one: a clean event taxonomy plus warehouse-grade analysis and attribution makes measurement a through-line that proves value across the whole engagement.
