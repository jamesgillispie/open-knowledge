# George (AREA 17 founder)

Founder, CEO, and strategic lead of [AREA 17](/clients/area-17/overview.md).

## Role

George is the firm's visionary and the author of its AI-era positioning. He drives the transformation narrative — strategy embedded in the brand, and the firm's repositioning for a world where AI reshapes how brands are encountered. James describes him as "a total visionary" who "raises the ceiling so high."

## The thesis: brand is behavior

George authored the behavioral-branding argument the firm is building its AI-era story around — the idea of an "intelligence fracture" and the core claim that **brand is behavior** (not just identity or messaging). His essay "Making Intelligence Human Again" carries the public-facing version of this thinking. This is the seed of the [AI's impact on brand & agency value](/concepts/behavioral-branding-ai.md) theme.

## What he does

- Authored the behavioral-branding thesis and the "Making Intelligence Human Again" piece.
- Secured as a speaker at South by Southwest (London) and the AI Summit; gave the SXSW London talk and presented it internally to the AREA 17 team.
- Drives the firm's [transformation / pods](/projects/a17-transformation.md) effort and AI-era positioning.

## Context

George sees James as bridging engineering and strategy, and has remarked that James felt "more strategist than engineer" — a read that connects to the [director overload / IC vs. strategist](/concepts/director-overload.md) tension James is navigating. George is also pushing the firm to prototype more (and get paid for it) as part of the [AI tooling initiative](/projects/ai-tooling-initiative.md).
