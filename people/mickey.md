# Mickey

Client-side web / UX and CMS lead at [Truth Initiative](/clients/truth-initiative/overview.md).

## Role

Mickey is the technical stakeholder and decision-maker AREA 17 wants the architecture follow-up with — the person who actually owns and builds the website inside the organization. In the pitch, the team deliberately leads the technical section by speaking to Mickey directly.

## What he does

- Owns the CMS and the website build on the client side; "landed strongly" as the technical stakeholder in the pitch room.
- Is the target of the deeper technical follow-up — the team agreed not to bury unreviewed technical detail in the appendix but to give Mickey real architecture detail directly.
- Surfaced the framing that what they've outgrown is the taxonomy and overall UI, and that a flexible, unopinionated, composable CMS is the goal.

## Context

Mickey's now-famous internal line captures why the technical pitch matters: *"everyone talked about brand and vision... and then I do the website."* The team uses this to justify leading with design governance and a tightly integrated design system — "that's why you're hiring us." See the [Truth Initiative pitch](/projects/truth-initiative-pitch.md). His framing connects to the [headless CMS hosting](/concepts/headless-cms-hosting.md) and [content migration & taxonomy](/concepts/content-migration-taxonomy.md) themes.
