# James Gillispie

**Role:** Engineering / Technical Director at [AREA 17](/clients/area-17/overview.md).

James is the owner of this vault and the note-taker behind every meeting and daily log it draws from. As TD he runs the technical workstream across the agency's most complex engagements simultaneously: the [Jane Street website redesign](/projects/jane-street-redesign.md), [Winston Taylor / Project Meridian](/projects/winston-taylor-mvp.md), the [NASEM site](/projects/nasem-site.md) care-and-growth program, the [Getty PST proposal](/projects/getty-pst-proposal.md), and a steady run of BD pitches.

## What he does

- **Technical direction across platforms.** Owns infrastructure and architecture decisions — CDN/edge config (Fastly through Upsun), CMS selection (Payload, Twill, headless), search relevance (OpenSearch/Meilisearch), and release engineering for large multi-branch deploys.
- **AI "Johnny Appleseed."** Self-described, James evangelizes Claude internally — "every time I meet with somebody" he shows them shared Claude projects, Claude Code, and agent workflows. He drives the firm's [AI tooling / internal agent initiative](/projects/ai-tooling-initiative.md) and the [FTZ analytics / GTM program](/projects/ftz-analytics.md). See [AI tooling adoption](/concepts/ai-tooling-adoption.md) and [behavioral branding / AI's impact on agency value](/concepts/behavioral-branding-ai.md).
- **Hands-on builder.** Vibe-codes and ships personal Laravel side projects — most notably **Manor House Gardens**, a custom journaling app he built over a weekend, used as a proving ground for AI-assisted coding.

## Context

A recurring theme across the notes is **overload** — James is consistently spread across multiple high-intensity projects at once (Meridian spanning California–Paris–UK time zones being the most punishing). The plan that emerges in spring 2026 is to hand Jane Street TD duties to [Patrick](/people/patrick-a17.md) (and later Tim) so James can concentrate on Winston Taylor and BD. See [director overload](/concepts/director-overload.md).

James previously worked at Athletics, which he describes as "not enough oxygen" — a low ceiling and excessive intensity — and is markedly happier in AREA 17's environment. He no longer drinks; coffee is, in his words, his remaining vice.
