# Nikki (Nicola)

**Role:** Senior producer / product director at [AREA 17](/clients/area-17/overview.md). Based in St. Pete, Florida.

Nikki is the most senior product director in the New York office and was identified in January 2026 as the best candidate to take over the [NASEM site](/projects/nasem-site.md) account as it transitioned from a launch project into an ongoing care-and-growth program — explicitly because of her technical knowledge and because the client (Alfonso's team) had asked for minimal team turnover. The model is "Pentagram-like": roughly 25% of her time managing backlog and growth sprints rather than a full build.

## What she does

- **Account leadership across a portfolio** — NASEM (care + growth sprints), Pentagram fractional team management, Lucas Museum, and inbound National Academies opportunities (NAE, TRB). The plan was for [Luke] to become her dedicated support partner across this portfolio once he rolled off Crystal Bridges care.
- **Producer/PM on [Jane Street](/projects/jane-street-redesign.md)** — runs client comms, confirms release scope, and owns URL-structure / project-consolidation reviews.
- **AI-assisted tooling.** Built a Claude-based Jira analytics dashboard and FigJam ticket breakdowns to give visibility into sprint health — a concrete example of the [AI tooling adoption](/concepts/ai-tooling-adoption.md) theme. Proposed a hierarchical search regression test suite for NASEM.
- **BD materials** — contributes to the [Getty PST proposal](/projects/getty-pst-proposal.md) and other proposal decks.

## Context

Nikki is careful about capacity — in the NASEM transition conversation she flagged that NASEM + BD + Lucas + Pentagram + internal time was already a heavy mix. She made the case (alongside [Ashley](/people/ashley.md)) for hiring a dedicated PM to sit "in the weeds" of tickets beneath her. See [sprint vs. retainer](/concepts/sprint-vs-retainer.md) and [director overload](/concepts/director-overload.md).
